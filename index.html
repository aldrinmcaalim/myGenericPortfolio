About Me

Hello there, my name is Aldrin. I'm the editor for "Probaby Programming" as well as a writer for "Level Up Coding" and "The Startup". All three are publications found on Medium. I enjoy sharing the knowledge I have gained with others through this platform. The first time I tried programming I was 18. I struggled to build up the habit, mostly due to the feeling of being a fraud. I thought to myself that this isn't for me, it's only for those with a computer science background. Every now and then my itch to program ignited. I'd got a week or two when the feeling of being a fraud hit me again. Then at the age of 21, I decided to set a goal to commit myself to programming daily, even if I felt like a fraud. My first program was a Rock, Paper, Scissors game. That's the first time I felt like I could do this. And now I've just been consistently building my skills, mostly by learning JavaScript. I've enjoyed the journey, whether I'm writing code, reading about code or writing about code. Each day I get to learn something new and enjoy sharing that with others. My new goal is to land a full-time job where I can help a company hit their goals.
