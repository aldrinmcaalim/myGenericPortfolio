About Me

Hello there, my name is Aldrin. I'm the editor for Probably Programming as well as a writer for Level Up Coding and The Startup. All three are publications found on Medium. I enjoy sharing the knowledge I have gained with others through this platform.

The first time I tried programming at 18. I struggled to build up the habit because I felt like a fraud. I believed that this wasn't for someone like me, it's only for those with a computer science background. But now and then my itch to program ignited. I'd give programming a go for a week or two until the feeling of being a fraud hit me again. Then at 21, I decided to set a goal to commit myself to programming daily, even if I felt like a fraud.

I ended up making a Rock, Paper, Scissors game. That was the first time I felt like I could do this. And now I've just been consistently building my skills, mostly by learning JavaScript. I've enjoyed the journey, whether I'm writing code, reading about code, or writing about code. Now each day I get to learn something new about the topic I love while also being able to share it with others. My new goal is to land a full-time job where I can help a company hit their goals.
